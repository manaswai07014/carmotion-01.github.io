## The Story

The headline from *TopGear* — Take cover! Aston Martin has built a massive, military-grade V12 supertruck - Top Gear — sets up a notable development in the car world. Here's the breakdown:

This is a massive, military-grade, zombie-proof combat truck powered by a V12 engine. It also has an Aston Martin badge on it (a company famous for its sleek supercars and grand tourers, not for securing MoD contracts) which is odd. And yes, we know it already makes the DBX SUV… but that’s nothing like this.

In other words, it’s called the Aston Martin Dreadnought, which a) suggests Aston may have run out of names beginning with V and b) is extremely cool, given it shares a name with the revolutionary HMS Dreadnought, that rewrote the rule book on naval warfare in the early 1900s. Although, we can’t help thinking Aston missed a trick not…

That is — it’s the latest in a long line of cartoonish, OEM-designed cars born for the digital world and freed from the constraints of physics. Most have appeared as Vision Gran Turismo concepts, often with physical models to back up the pixels. Stuff like the Bugatti Vision GT that previewed the Chiron, the 400mph 2,500bhp Dodge SRT…

To put it simply, it’s a collaboration between the developers of the new Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4 game and Aston’s own design department, so you’ll be able to ‘drive’ it when the game launches in October. In the meantime, they’ve built a physical model, because why the hell not.

Breaking this down, it has enormous off-road tyres, more ground clearance than a Unimog, a reserve fuel tank, bespoke weapons storage, enough spotlights to illuminate Wembley Stadium, military-grade armour plating and ‘adaptive combat zone intelligence systems’ whatever those are. So far so warzone-ready, and yet there’s herringbone…

Now, you’ll note there’s no actual interior on the model, but we do have some in-game renders of the surprisingly luxurious insides. Lots of leather, a long sweep of screen for all your… tactical stuff, and chunky physical controls in the centre console along with removable storage.
